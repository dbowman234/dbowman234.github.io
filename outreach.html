Outreach

I am passionate about public engagement and outreach in science, but particularly in astronomy. Throughout my research career, I have organised and assisted in many observing sessions for primary and secondary school students, scout and guide groups (etc.), and amateur astronomy societies. This included using the modern 0.7-m telescope at UCLan’s Alston observatory, at which visitors were given an interactive tour of the night sky using the modern planetarium. I have also visited primary and secondary school students in their classrooms to give talks and run astronomy-themed group activities. These are interactive and tailor-made to support the astronomy section of the national curriculum for students between the ages of 4 and 18. It is highly enjoyable and rewarding to engage with young students and members of the public and discuss astronomy at various levels. I am dedicated to continue participating and organising similar outreach activities throughout my research career.

Below are a selection of some of the outreach activities I have carried out at KU Leuven:

For Schools and Pupils:

  • I am an ongoing participant of the KU Leuven Scientist at School program, in which I visit local schools and provide astronomy-themed lectures and workshops.
  • A 90-min workshop on space exploration and the solar system at the KU Leuven Kids University, for 25 students aged 8–12 on 5th May 2018.
  • 1-hr talks on `Stellar Clusters' and `Variable Stars' at the Vereniging Voor Sterrenkunde Zomerschool, hosted at KU Leuven for 30-40 students aged 16-18 in August 2017, 2018 and 2020.
  • Two 90-min workshops on `Exoplanets, Habibility and Host Star Variability' for the Ladies@Science event, hosted at KU Leuven for 40 students on 19th April 2017.

For the General Public:

  • A series of short popular-science videos in collaboration with Huawei and Pint of Science Belgium featured on the ``5-minute science you never knew'' YouTube channel.
  • I was an invited speaker for the Belgian Pint of Science events in Brussels on 7 and 21 May 2019, and gave fun talks themed on my research work for a broad public audience.
  • Co-author of Dutch article for the September 2019 issue of the popular astronomy magazine Heelal.
  • I have also helped organise and assisted the Institute of Astronomy's contribution to KU Leuven open days for members of the public and prospective undergraduate students.
