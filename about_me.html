About Me

I was born in the United Kingdom and hold both British and Irish citizenship. I went to high school in Preston between 2002 and 2007, graduating with the James Henderson trophy for academic achievement. I also attended college in Preston between 2007 and 2009 and studied Physics, Maths, Chemistry and Biology at GCE A-level. Between 2009 and 2013, I was an undergraduate at the University of Birmingham and attained a first-class with honours Master of Science (MSci) in Physics and Astrophysics in July 2013. I completed my PhD in astronomy at the University of Central Lancashire in November 2016, which was funded by the UK Science and Technology Facilities Council (STFC). Since 2017, I live and work in Leuven, Belgium as a postdoctoral research fellow at KU Leuven.

Research Network

I currently hold leading roles in several international consortia, including MOBSTER, CubeSpec , XShootU, BEST, and Arago, which are focussed on the study of massive stars using a variety of astronomical techniques. I am a fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society (FRAS), a Member of the Institute of Physics (MInstP), a member of the European Astronomical Society (EAS) and a junior member of the International Astronomical Union (IAU). These professional organisations allow me to network with other researchers in physics and astronomy. They also organise scientific meetings, training and mentoring workshops, which I have attended to improve my communication, outreach and career prospects, and pass my knowledge and experience onto others.

Between 2020 and 2023, I was the Secretary of the UK Royal Astronomical Society (RAS) Early Career Network (ECN). We organised several peer mentoring, training and advocacy workshops, which have led to several publications in the RAS's Astronomy & Geophysics (A&G) journal. As an example, I am the leading author on the A&G articles summarising the ECN's 'How to find your first postdoc' and 'PhD mentoring' events.

Important Achievements

In October 2017, my PhD thesis was published as a Springer monograph. In May 2019, I published a first-author paper in Nature Astronomy on part of my postdoctoral research on gravity waves at KU Leuven, which was selected as the cover image for the August 2019 issue. In May 2020, I was awarded the prestigious KU Leuven Research Council Award in Science, Engineering and Technology (2020) for my breakthrough research in asteroseismology of massive stars, which is an annual prize awarded to only a single postdoctoral researcher. In December 2022, I won the Henri Vanderlinden Prize from the Royal Flemish Academy of Belgium for Science and the Arts for an outstanding piece of original research work in astronomy. In January 2023, I was awarded the 2023 George Darwin Lectureship of the Royal Astronomical Society (RAS), for being an authoritative and engaging researcher and communicator of astronomy.

In recent news: I have been awarded a prestigous ERC Starting Grant for my SYMPHONY project, which will combine TESS photometry and spectroscopy, including from the ESA/KU Leuven CubeSpec space mission, to perform asteroseismology of massive stars and constrain their uncertain interior physical processes.
