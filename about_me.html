About Me

I was born on 15th October 1990 in the United Kingdom and went to high school in Preston, Lancashire between 2002 and 2007, graduating with the James Henderson trophy for academic achievement. I also attended college in Preston between 2007 and 2009 and studied Physics, Maths, Chemistry and Biology at GCE A-level. Between 2009 and 2013, I was an undergraduate at the University of Birmingham and attained a first-class honours Master of Science (MSci) in Physics and Astrophysics in July 2013. I completed my PhD in astronomy at the University of Central Lancashire in November 2016, which was funded by the UK Science and Technology Facilities Council (STFC). In 2017, my thesis was selected to be published by Springer.

I am a fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society (FRAS), a Member of the Institute of Physics (MInstP), and a member of the European Astronomical Society (EAS). These professional organisations allow me to meet other researchers in physics and astronomy. They also organise scientific meetings, media training workshops and career days, which I have attended to improve my communication and outreach skills.

In my spare time, I enjoy reading (especially classic science fiction) and also enjoy hiking, swimming, climbing, and playing badminton and squash with friends and colleagues. Of course, since I work at the KU Leuven I also enjoy cycling, which is a must for someone living in Belgium!
